Composition

Composition is the foundation of a good photograph. It’s how you arrange elements in the frame to guide the viewer’s eye and create a balanced image. A strong composition can evoke emotion, tell a story, or simply create a visually appealing image.

Rule of Thirds

The rule of thirds divides the image into a 3x3 grid. Place important elements along these lines or at their intersections for balance and interest.

Leading Lines

Leading lines are lines that direct the viewer's gaze. Roads, rivers, or architectural elements guide the eye to a focal point.

Framing

Framing uses natural elements to create a "frame" around the subject, isolating it and drawing attention.

Lighting

Lighting is crucial to photography. It defines the mood, depth, and quality of your image. Whether natural or artificial, soft or hard, lighting changes the feel of your photo.

Natural Light

The golden hours—just after sunrise and before sunset—provide soft, warm light for portraits and landscapes.

Hard vs. Soft Light

Hard light casts sharp shadows, while soft light creates a more diffused, flattering effect.

Backlighting

Backlighting places the light source behind the subject, often creating silhouettes or dramatic contrast.

Exposure Triangle

The exposure triangle is a key concept that balances aperture, shutter speed, and ISO. Mastering it allows for the right exposure in any situation.

Aperture (f-stop)

A lower f-stop blurs the background, while a higher f-stop increases the depth of field.

Shutter Speed

Shutter speed affects motion. Fast speeds freeze action; slower speeds create motion blur.

ISO

ISO adjusts the camera’s sensitivity to light. Use low ISO for bright conditions, and higher ISO for low-light situations.

Editing

Editing is where creativity meets precision. Enhance the best aspects of your photo or correct issues with tools like exposure adjustments and color correction.

Color Correction

Adjust the colors to look more natural, fixing color casts introduced by lighting.

Exposure Adjustments

Fine-tune the brightness, contrast, and shadows to balance the overall exposure of your image.

Retouching

Remove imperfections like dust spots or blemishes to create a polished, professional image.

Storytelling

Photography is a powerful storytelling tool. Each photo can evoke emotion, provide insight into a subject, and transport the viewer into a different world.

Emotional Impact

Capture raw emotions to connect with viewers on a deeper level and tell the subject's story.

Context & Environment

Use the background and environment to add layers of meaning to your photograph.

Juxtaposition

Place contrasting elements side by side to create tension and tell a more complex story.
